    The ten commandments of a good dental practice.

    1. The patient is the most important person to your practice.

    2. A patient is not dependent on you, you are dependent on them.

    3. A patient is not an interruption to your work, they are the purpose of it.

    4. A patient is a part of our practice, not an outsider.

    5. A patient does a favor when they call; we are not doing them a favor by just seeing them.

    6. A patient is not a cold statistic; they are a human being with feelings and emotions just like our own.

    7. A patient is not someone to argue with.

    8. A patient is one who brings us needs and wants, and it is our job to fulfill those needs.

    9. A patient deserves the most courteous and attentive treatment that we can give.

    10. A patient is the lifeblood of our profession.
